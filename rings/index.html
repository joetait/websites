Examples of Rings that lack an identity element

\(n \mathbb Z \) for any \( n \in \mathbb N_{>1} \).

\( C_0(X) \) for any locally compact non-compact space \( X \).

Given any ring \( R \) (unital or otherwise) we can form the non-unital ring \( \left\{ \left. \begin{pmatrix} 0 & a_{1,2} & a_{1,3} & \cdots & a_{1,n} \\ 0 & 0 & a_{2,3} &\cdots & a_{2,n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \end{pmatrix} \right| a_{i,j} \in R\right\} \)

\( R[x] \) for any non-unital ring \(R\)
